About Company Page


“Anubhooti solutions” is engaged in working in the area of speech processing. Dr. Shaila D. Apte is a proprietor of the firm. Projects completed: POC for natural sounding emotional speech synthesis in English using phoneme concatenation and with minimal recording form the layman. Projects working on: Emotion recognition useful for call centers, Natural sounding and emotional speech synthesis in Marathi and Hindi with minimal recording from the speaker, Speaker authentication using optimal mother wavelet, Speech enhancement using conjugate property of DFT and wavelet Transform, Speech disorder classification.

Dr. Mrs. S. D. Apte completed her ME and Ph. D (Electronics Engineering) from Walchand College of Engineering, Sangli. She has professional experience of 35 years. She worked in Walchand College of Engineering for 27 years and currently working as a Professor for PG in Rajarshi Shahu College of Engineering, Pune. She has two Patents to her credit related to generation of mother wavelet from speech signal and emotional speech synthesis using modification of pitch contour and LPCs. She has published 40 research papers in reputed International journals and over 40 papers in International conferences. Eight candidates have completed Ph.D. under her guidance ad she is currently guiding 5 Ph.D. students in Pune University. She is currently working as a reviewer for International Journal of Speech Technology, Springer Publication and International Journal on Signal Processing, Elesevier Publication. Books titled “Digital Signal Processing”, “Speech and Audio Processing” and “Advanced Digital Signal Processing” is published by Wiley India Publication. “Signals and Systems, principles and applications” is recently published by Cambridge University press. “Random Signal Processing” title will be published by CRC press, Taylor and Francis series. Her research areas include speaker verification, speech recognition, speech synthesis, speech enhancement, watermarking of speech and music signals, handwritten character recognition etc.
